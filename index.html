The Process

Four steps to your first print

01

Coat

Brush the light-sensitive emulsion onto paper, fabric, or any absorbent surface. Work in dim light.

02

Compose

Arrange botanicals, objects, or transparent negatives on your coated surface. This is your image.

03

Expose

Place under direct sunlight or UV light. The chemistry reacts where light hits — patience is the developer.

04

Rinse

Wash in water. Unexposed chemistry dissolves, revealing your image in deep Prussian blue.

About Cyanite

Analog process,
contemporary practice

Cyanotype was invented in 1842 by Sir John Herschel. Anna Atkins used it to catalog botanical specimens — creating what many consider the first photobook. We teach this 180-year-old process because it still surprises.

No darkroom. No expensive equipment. Just iron salts, sunlight, and water. The simplicity is the point — it strips image-making back to its essence and lets you focus on seeing, composing, and being patient with light.
