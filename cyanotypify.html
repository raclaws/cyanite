Methodology

This filter simulates cyanotype printing using a first-principles photochemical model, not an arbitrary color overlay.

  • Characteristic curve — Tone mapping follows the S-shaped response curve of iron-salt photochemistry: quadratic toe (shadow compression), linear midtones, quadratic shoulder (highlight compression). Shadows are bounded by Dmax (maximum Prussian blue density), highlights by Dmin (paper tone).
  • Exposure — Shifts the curve along the log-exposure axis, simulating UV duration (20min → 150min+). Higher exposure pushes more tonal values toward blue.
  • Paper tone — Models the physical substrate (cotton rag / watercolor paper). Warm cream base, adjustable via the tone slider to simulate different paper stocks.
  • Vignette — Simulates uneven hand-coating of emulsion, where edges receive less chemistry and therefore less density.
  • Chemical bleed — Gaussian softness models how iron salts diffuse into paper fibers during development, preventing razor-sharp edges.
  • Paper texture — Layered noise at two scales (fine grain + fiber strands) simulates watercolor paper fiber showing through the emulsion.

No machine learning, no server processing, no tokens. Pure deterministic image processing running entirely in your browser via the Canvas API.
